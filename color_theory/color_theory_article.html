Color is a powerful tool in web design, as it can help convey a message, create visual interest, and guide usersâ€™ attention to key elements on a page. Thoughtful use of color can enhance user experience and even influence how users perceive a brand or message.
For example, the Beans Agency website (see screenshot below)effectively uses vibrant colors and dynamic animations to draw visitors in and encourage them to keep scrolling. The use of full-screen, monotone color blocks for each section creates a visually immersive experience, making it easy for users to focus on the content within each section. The simplicity of the color scheme, combined with animation, creates an engaging and seamless flow from one section to the next, helping maintain user interest throughout the page.
In contrast, the Berkshire Hathaway website (see screenshot below)features a very basic, minimalist design. The white background paired with single-colored text gives the site a more traditional, straightforward look. While this approach may work for certain audiences, it lacks the visual appeal and interactive elements that could help capture attention or encourage deeper engagement with the content. The design feels somewhat static and less dynamic compared to more modern, color-rich websites, potentially making it less engaging for users.
Ultimately, the strategic use of color can greatly enhance a website's aesthetic appeal and functionality, making it more engaging and user-friendly, while a more basic design may work for some brands but risk being less captivating to others.
