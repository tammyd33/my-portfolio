Color theory is the study of how colors interact with each other and how they affect peopleâ€™s emotions and perceptions. Colors can draw attention, make you feel a certain way, and impact your design's overall message. Below are some common terms related to color theory.

Some common terms include:

  1. Color Wheel – a fundamental tool in color theory which illustrates the relationship between colors
  2. Primary Colors – colors that are pure and cannot be created by mixing other colors: red, blue, and yellow
  3. Secondary Colors – colors that are formed by mixing two primary colors: green, violet, and orange
  4. Tertiary Colors – colors that are a result of mixing a primary color with a secondary color: blue-violet, blue-green, yellow-green, yellow-orange, red-orange, and red-violet
  5. Saturation – the degree of purity and brightness within a color
  6. Hue – the purest form of a color
  7. Shade – a hue with black added to darken the color
  8. Tint – a hue with white added to lighten the color
  9. Tone – a hue with grey added to give a muted color
  10. Value of Hue – the relative lightness or darkness of a color
  11. Color Scheme – a set of colors, properly labeled and made available to everyone involved with a project in order to ensure a cohesive look and feel
  12. Monochromatic Colors Scheme – a color palette that uses shades and tints of a single color to created a unified look
  13. Triadic Color Scheme – a color palette that uses three colors that are evenly spaced around the color wheel to form a triangle
  14. Complementary Color Scheme – a color palette that uses colors that are opposite each other on the color wheel
  15. Split Color Scheme – a color palette that uses a base color and two colors that would be balanced from the opposite side of the color wheel
  16. Analogous Color Scheme – colors that are next to each other on the color wheel
  17. White Space – the empty space between and around design elements and text, also called negative space, that can be any color, texture, or pattern
  18. RGB – stands for red, green, and blue and it is a color mode used by screens to build a light-based color system
  19. CMYK – stands for cyan, magenta, yellow, and black and are colored inks used in the printing process
  20. Contrast – the difference between the lightest and darkest tones in an image
  21. Vibrancy – in color theory this means a lively, bright, rich, energetic hue
