Typography is more than just how a font looks, it is also a way to guide readers along a page.

Some common typography terms used in design include:

  1. Typeface
    a specific design of letters and numbers, either in print or on a computer
  2. Font
    a particular size, weight and style of a typeface
  3. OpenType
    a universal file format designed for computer-based typefaces
  4. Serif
    corners, or “feet”, that exist on the end of the strokes of a typeface
  5. Sans-serif
    a typeface designed without serifs
  6. Decorative
    a typeface with a unique shape and personality that is used to grab attention
  7. Script
    a style of font that imitates the look of handwritten or calligraphic writing
  8. Mono-spaced
    a typeface where all characters, including letters, numbers, symbols, and spaces, take up the same amount of horizontal space
  9. Layout
    the strategic arrangement of text and other elements on a page or screen
  10. Typography Hierarchy
    the visual organization of written content to indicate the relative importance of its elements
