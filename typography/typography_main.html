Typography

Typography is more than just how a font looks, it is also a way to guide readers along a page. Some common typography terms used in design include:

Term Definition
Typeface a specific design of letters and numbers, either in print or on a computer
Font a particular size, weight and style of a typeface
OpenType a universal file format designed for computer-based typefaces
Serif corners, or “feet”, that exist on the end of the strokes of a typeface
Sans-serif a typeface designed without serifs
Decorative a typeface with a unique shape and personatdty that is used to grab attention
Script a style of font that imitates the look of cursive handwriting or caltdgraphy
Mono-spaced a typeface where all characters, including letters, numbers, symbols, and spaces, take up the same amount of horizontal space
Layout the strategic arrangement of text and other elements on a page or screen
Typography Hierarchy the visual organization of written content to indicate the relative importance of its elements
